Biryani

Biryani is a celebrated South Asian dish of tender marinated meat (or vegetables) and fragrant, long-grained rice. A classic biryani is an aromatic, layered culinary masterpiece, characterized by:

This universally popular dish is characterized by several key features:

  • Deep flavor: The rice and meat are cooked in layers, absorbing the complex flavors of saffron, fried onions (birista), and a delicate blend of warm spices like cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon.

  • Distinct texture: The dish achieves its signature quality through the dum pukht method, or slow-steam cooking, which results in fluffy, separate rice grains and succulent, fall-off-the-bone meat.

  • Mesmerizing aroma: A thick yogurt marinade helps tenderize the chicken, keeping it moist throughout the high-heat cooking process.

Tandoori chicken recipe (oven method)

This recipe uses a simple two-stage marination process for maximum flavor and tenderness.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (about 2.2 lbs) chicken drumsticks or thighs, skin removed
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup thick plain yogurt (or Greek yogurt)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

Steps

  1. Prepare the chicken. Clean the chicken pieces thoroughly and pat them completely dry with paper towels. Make several deep cuts or slits in the meat to allow the marinade to penetrate.
  2. Apply the first marinade. In a bowl, rub the chicken pieces with lemon juice and salt. Let it rest for at least 20 minutes.
  3. Make the second marinade. In a large bowl, whisk together the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, oil, and all the spices (chili powder, garam masala, cumin, coriander, turmeric, kasuri methi, black pepper, and salt) until it forms a thick, smooth paste. Add food coloring now if you are using it.
  4. Marinate the chicken. Drain any excess liquid from the first marinade. Add the chicken pieces to the yogurt marinade and rub it in thoroughly, making sure it gets into all the slits. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4–6 hours, or overnight for the best flavor.
  5. Preheat the oven. When ready to cook, remove the chicken from the fridge and let it sit for 30 minutes. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F) and line a baking sheet with foil, placing a wire rack on top.
